Our Story

Born from
Obsession

Malabar & Co. began with a single bag of Java Malabar beans and a conviction that coffee could be more than a morning ritual — it could be a genuine sensory journey.

We source directly from farmers in Indonesia, Ethiopia, and Colombia. Each batch is roasted in-house to a profile we've spent years perfecting, then crafted by hand in every cup we serve.

From the velvety texture of our signature milk foam to the precise 93°C extraction of our espressos — every element is intentional.

The Craft

Milk, Grind
& Fire

01

Bean Selection

We taste every lot before we buy. Only single-origin beans that pass our rigorous cupping standards make it to your cup.

02

Precision Roasting

Our drum roaster runs at precise profiles for each origin. Java Malabar demands a dark city roast — 220°C for 14 minutes, drum-cooled immediately.

03

Grind to Order

Every shot is ground fresh. Particle size is calibrated to the humidity of the day — a barista decision, not a machine default.

04

Milk Texturing

Full-cream milk is steamed to 63–67°C with precise wand technique, creating a glossy microfoam with zero bubbles — just silk.

05

Serve with Intent

Each drink leaves the bar within 30 seconds of the final pour. Your cup is always the warmest thing in the room.

Single Origins

Sourced with Purpose

We travel, we cup, we build relationships. These three origins are the backbone of our menu — each one irreplaceable.

🇮🇩

Java Malabar

West Java, Indonesia

Processed by the unique wet-hulling (Giling Basah) method indigenous to Indonesia. This creates the signature syrupy body, earthy mushroom notes, and low acidity that Java Malabar is celebrated for. Pairs beautifully with dark chocolate.

1,400 – 1,800m altitude Dark City Roast
🇪🇹

Yirgacheffe Natural

Southern Ethiopia

Sun-dried on raised beds for 21 days, imparting an intensely fruity, wine-like complexity. Blueberry, bergamot, and florals dominate — Ethiopia's gift to the world of specialty coffee.

1,700 – 2,200m altitude Light Roast
🇨🇴

Huila Washed

Huila Department, Colombia

Washed and fermented for 36 hours before drying on parabolic beds. Clean, bright, with caramel sweetness and notes of red apple and brown sugar. Our espresso base blend.

1,600 – 2,000m altitude Medium Roast
